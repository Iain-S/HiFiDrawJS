Example 1 - Balanced Active 2.1 Setup

Here we see how you could wire together:

  • a Focusrite Scarlett 2i2
  • a JBL LSR310 subwoofer
  • two JBL LSR305 active bookshelf speakers

It would work equally well with:

  • other balanced active monitors (such as the Genelec 8010A)
  • any soundcard with a balanced output (e.g. the Steinberg UR22mkII)
  • other sub-woofers with balanced inputs and outputs (e.g. the M-Audio BX Sub)

Example 2 - Unbalanced Passive 2.1 Setup

Option 1

With this option, the subwoofer is connected to the amp with a separate cable run. This might allow you to more easily reposition the subwoofer as the placement is limited only by the speaker cable and the location of the amp.

There is no way for the sub to alter the signal going to the speakers so all it can do is to "fill in" the lower octave or two. Good integration relies on careful adjustment of the sub high-pass cutoff frequency and the phase.
